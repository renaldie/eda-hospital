# Post-Discharge Nursing Guidance for Victims of Sexual Assault

1. Please monitor whether your menstrual cycle is normal and watch for signs of pregnancy, such as nausea, vomiting, or delayed menstruation.

2. Please be alert for signs of infection, such as fever, lower abdominal pain, itching in the perineal area, burning sensation in the perineal area, or abnormal discharge.

3. Our hospital has conducted tests for sexually transmitted diseases and HIV for you. Please attend your scheduled follow-up appointment as per the appointment time to confirm the results and proceed with further treatment.

4. If you experience feelings of depression, helplessness, fear, anxiety, or pain, you may call the "113 Women and Children Protection Hotline" or contact the "Family Violence and Sexual Assault Prevention Center" of the municipal or county (city) government in person. Social workers will be available to provide assistance.

5. Support Channels:

(1) If you have any questions, please contact the social worker at the prevention center for inquiries
