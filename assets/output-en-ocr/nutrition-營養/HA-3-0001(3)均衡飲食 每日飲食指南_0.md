## [National Dietary Indicators]

1. Diet should be based on the food categories and recommended portions in the "Daily Dietary Guide," with appropriate selection and combination. Pay special attention to consuming sufficient amounts of vegetables, fruits, whole grains, legumes, nuts and seeds, and dairy products.

2. Understand your healthy body weight and caloric needs, and eat in moderation to maintain a healthy weight within the normal range.

3. Maintain an active lifestyle, accumulating at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity physical activity per week, or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity physical activity.

4. Breastfeed infants for at least six months, and then introduce appropriate complementary foods.

5. Main meals should be based on whole grains and coarse grains.

6. Eat more vegetables and less red meat; consume more unrefined foods and less refined foods.

7. Diversify your diet and choose seasonal, local ingredients.

8. When purchasing food or ordering meals, pay attention to portion sizes to avoid overeating or food waste.

9. Try to minimize consumption of fried and high-fat, high-sugar foods, and avoid sugary beverages.

10. Maintain a light flavor; avoid excessive salt intake, limit pickled foods, and consume condiments in moderation.

11. If drinking alcohol, men should not exceed 2 servings per day, and women should not exceed 1 serving per day. Alcohol consumption is strictly prohibited during pregnancy.

Note: 1 serving = 10 grams of alcohol, such as: 250 ml of beer, 100 ml of red or white wine, 75 ml of shochu, 60 ml of rice wine, 30 ml of brandy or whisky, 20 ml of grain alcohol, etc.

12. Choose foods with clear origin labeling and those that are hygienic and safe.

References: National Health Agency, Taiwan, "Daily Dietary Guide," "National Dietary Indicators," and "My Plate: Smart Eating for Nutrition and Health," 2018.
