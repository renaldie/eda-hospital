## Urological Stones

## I. Causes of ureteral stones:

High concentrations of various free ions in urine lead to crystal aggregation and stone formation.

## II. Symptoms of urological stones:

1. Dull pain in the back or renal colic, which may radiate to the testicles (in males) or bladder (in females).

2. Infection: chills or fever.

3. Hematuria, urgency, and difficulty in urination.

4. Possible obstruction leading to renal edema, urinary retention, and impaired renal function.

## III. Treatment of urological stones: Treatment options must be evaluated by a physician

1. Extracorporeal shock wave lithotripsy (ESWL).

2. Endoscopic stone removal is preferred.

3. Percutaneous nephrostomy surgery.

3. Urinate naturally and avoid holding urine.

4. Seek early treatment if urine color darkens or urination becomes difficult.

5. Engage in appropriate leisure activities.
