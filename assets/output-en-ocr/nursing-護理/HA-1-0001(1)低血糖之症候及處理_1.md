## Symptoms and Management of Hypoglycemia

## One: What is Hypoglycemia?

When blood glucose levels fall below 50 mg/dL or when symptoms such as cold sweats, shakiness, or confusion appear, it is referred to as hypoglycemia.

## Two: Causes of Hypoglycemia

1. Excess insulin or hypoglycemic medication.

2. Eating nothing after taking hypoglycemic medication or insulin injections.

3. Increased physical activity without additional food intake.

4. Impaired liver or kidney function.

## Two: Symptoms of Hypoglycemia

1. Autonomic symptoms: Early signs of hypoglycemia include shakiness, cold sweats, palpitations, and an accelerated heartbeat.

2. Central nervous system symptoms: Drowsiness, confusion, seizures, and coma.

3. Non-specific symptoms: Headache, dizziness, nausea, numbness in the lips, etc.

## Three: Prevention of Hypoglycemia

1. Eat regular, measured meals and snacks at three main meals and snacks.

2. Remember the warning signs of hypoglycemia.

3. Avoid exercising on an empty stomach.

4. Take oral medications on time and avoid long intervals between medication and meals.

5. Accurately administer insulin injections, and consume food within 30 minutes after injection.

6. Carry candy or biscuits at all times, and keep a diabetes patient identification card ready.

7. If blood glucose is below 110 mg/dL before bedtime, consume a snack.

8. For patients with "non-alert hypoglycemia," avoid overly strict glucose control.

## Four: Management before medical help during hypoglycemic coma

1. Do not administer insulin.

2. Do not force food into the patient.

3. Keep the airway clear.

4. Call emergency services at 119.

## Five: Treatment of Hypoglycemia

1. If the patient is conscious, consume 4 to 6 pieces of sugar cubes. If symptoms do not improve after 10 to 15 minutes, consume another dose (or 120 to 180 cc of juice or one tablespoon of honey).

2. If the patient is unconscious, immediately take them to the hospital for treatment.
