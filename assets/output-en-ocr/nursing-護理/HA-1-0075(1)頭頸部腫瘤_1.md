## Head and Neck Tumors

## One: What are head and neck tumors?

Head and neck tumors refer to diseases caused by abnormal cellular changes and rapid differentiation in organs of the head and neck region. Head and neck tumors can be benign or malignant, and they encompass areas such as the face, nasopharynx, nasal cavity, oral cavity, pharynx, and neck. These tumors can affect an individual's ability to communicate, eat, swallow, and even breathe, to varying degrees. To minimize unnecessary harm to the body, it is essential to properly care for head and neck-related conditions.

## Two: Causes of head and neck tumors:

1. Regional differences: Due to differences in regional characteristics, certain diseases are more prevalent in specific areas. For example, Chinese people are more prone to nasopharyngeal tumors.

2. Viral infection: Commonly, Epstein-Barr virus (EBV).

3. Dietary habits: Salted food.

4. Smoking.

5. Alcohol consumption.

6. Chewing betel nut.

## Three: Classification of head and neck tumors:

1. Classified by cellular morphology based on tissue biopsy results: Squamous epithelial cell type and columnar epithelial cell type.

2. Classified by anatomical site: Nasopharynx, oral region (including tongue, cheeks, and jaw), oropharynx (including base of tongue and tonsils), hypopharynx, and larynx.

## Four: Symptoms of head and neck tumors:

1. Presence of varying-sized mass-like protrusions in the neck.

2. Oral tumors: Local swelling or ulcers, mucosal ulcers that do not heal, and pain.

3. Pharyngeal tumors: Difficulty swallowing and local pain.

4. Nasal and nasopharyngeal tumors: Nasal discharge with blood streaks, nasal congestion, decreased hearing, facial swelling causing pain, diplopia, tinnitus, headache, and epistaxis.

5. Hypopharyngeal tumors: Difficulty swallowing and presence of neck masses.

6. Laryngeal tumors: Local burning sensation, pain, difficulty swallowing, breathing difficulties, stridor, chronic pain, hemoptysis, and hoarseness.

## Five: Treatment methods for head and neck tumors:

1. Surgical treatment.

2. Chemotherapy.

3. Radiotherapy.
