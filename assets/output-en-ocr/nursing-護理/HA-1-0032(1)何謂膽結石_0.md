Can be diagnosed with computed tomography (CT) or retrograde cholangiopancreatography (ERCP).

## Six. Treatment:

1. Pain relief: This is the priority. Smooth muscle relaxants, non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs), and analgesics are effective; however, morphine is not recommended, as it causes sphincter of Oddi spasm, increasing pressure in the common bile duct and worsening pain.

2. Removal of the hair follicle cyst is the most effective treatment for hair follicle cyst stones.

3. Cholelithiasis dissolution therapy is an alternative approach for treating cholesterol stones.
