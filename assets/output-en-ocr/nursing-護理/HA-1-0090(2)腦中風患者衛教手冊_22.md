## Daily Living Habitation

<div style="text-align: center;"><img src="imgs/img_in_image_box_0_203_57_306.jpg" alt="Image" width="6%" /></div>

There should be stable and reliable handrails or furniture, as well as related accessible facilities both indoors and outdoors to prevent falls.

<div style="text-align: center;"><img src="imgs/img_in_image_box_0_394_69_499.jpg" alt="Image" width="7%" /></div>

The bed should not be too high, allowing the patient to step down without having their feet dangling in the air, so they can safely touch the ground.

<div style="text-align: center;"><img src="imgs/img_in_image_box_0_565_51_670.jpg" alt="Image" width="5%" /></div>

The home environment should have adequate lighting to avoid poor visibility and falls.

<div style="text-align: center;"><img src="imgs/img_in_image_box_593_23_874_297.jpg" alt="Image" width="32%" /></div>

<div style="text-align: center;"><img src="imgs/img_in_image_box_465_318_547_412.jpg" alt="Image" width="9%" /></div>

Furniture arrangements in the room should be kept as stable and unchanged as possible.

<div style="text-align: center;"><img src="imgs/img_in_image_box_478_487_547_583.jpg" alt="Image" width="7%" /></div>

The bathroom should have handrails and non-slip facilities, and using a sitting toilet is safer.
