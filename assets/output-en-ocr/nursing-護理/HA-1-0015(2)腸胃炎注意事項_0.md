Six. If any of the following situations occur, please return for a follow-up visit immediately:

1. Persistent nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea becoming increasingly frequent.

2. If stool contains blood streaks or mucus, this may indicate bacterial gastroenteritis; seek medical attention, and ideally bring a sample of the stool to the doctor for examination.

3. If a persistent fever exceeding $ 38.5^{\circ} $ C, double vision, seizures, or paralysis occurs.

4. Severe abdominal pain, especially if the location or nature of the pain changes, requires immediate medical attention.

5. Signs of dehydration, such as intense thirst, dry and loss of elasticity in the skin, dark-colored urine, or reduced urine output.
