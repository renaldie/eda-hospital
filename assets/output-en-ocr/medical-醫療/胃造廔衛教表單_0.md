# Percutaneous Endoscopic Gastrostomy (PEG) Post-Procedure Care Instructions

## 1. Definitions

Patients may require tube feeding due to swallowing difficulties caused by certain diseases. A gastrostomy is an alternative to nasogastric tubes, involving the creation of a stoma on the stomach and abdominal wall, followed by the insertion of a feeding tube.

## 2. General Applicable Cases

1. Patients with stroke.

2. Patients with swallowing difficulties after treatment for head and neck cancer or esophageal cancer.

3. Patients with brain lesions or neuromuscular disorders requiring long-term nasogastric tube placement.

4. Patients who cannot have a nasogastric tube placed.
