# IVF Treatment Explanation

The artificial reproduction law stipulates that only married couples are eligible to receive artificial reproduction technology. Individuals wishing to undergo treatment must confirm that they have completed marriage registration.

## (1) Examination Items

| Time | Process | Description | Notes |
|------|--------|-------------|-------|
| | ○ Sperm analysis | Confirm sperm condition | |
| Menstrual cycle day 2-3 | Ovarian hormone test | Confirm ovarian function; blood tests for E2, LH, FSH, TSH, AMH, Prolactin, etc. | |
| Days 3-5 after menstruation ends (row span 2) | Hysteroscopy (arranged by physician as needed) | Confirm uterine condition; the hysteroscopy procedure does not require anesthesia. If abnormalities are found, additional surgery is required. Surgery is performed under general anesthesia and requires a 3-day, 2-night hospital stay. | Surgery, anesthesia, and hospitalization fees are extra |
| | Tubal patency test | Confirm tubal patency; first perform hysteroscopy, then tubal patency test. The tubal patency test requires fasting (including water) for 4 hours. | |

## (2) Treatment Items

| Time | Process | Description | Notes |
|------|--------|-------------|-------|
| Menstrual cycle day 2-3 | Administer ovulation-inducing injection | Stimulate follicle growth; before administering the injection, perform vaginal ultrasound and blood tests (CBC, VDRL, HIV, E2, FSH, LH). Perform pre-egg retrieval checks (1): ECG and X-ray examination. | |
| Days 3-14 | Administer ovulation-inducing injection | Stimulate follicle growth; continuously administer FSH injections for 8-12 days (up to 14 days) to promote follicle development. Store medication in the refrigerator; do not place in the freezer. Follow physician's instructions to return to the hospital for vaginal ultrasound monitoring of follicle maturity, approximately 3-4 times. | Fees vary depending on drug type, formulation, and duration of use |
| Day 15 | Trigger ovulation injection | Promote follicle maturation to achieve optimal egg retrieval timing; return to the hospital to collect the trigger injection (hCG). Ovulation induction is typically performed when follicles reach ≥1.8 cm in diameter. | |
