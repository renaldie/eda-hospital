# INTRAOCULAR INJECTION CARE GUIDELINE AND HEALTH EDUCATION SHEET

## I. Definition of Intraocular Injection

Injecting medication into the vitreous body of the eye to inhibit neovascularization and macular lesions, or to treat endophthalmitis.

## II. Applicable Population

Patients with diabetic macular edema and retinal disease.

## III. Pre-injection Precautions

1. The doctor will assess the severity of the eye condition, explain the injection procedure, expected outcomes, and costs.

2. Patients must complete the surgical consent form and self-paid service consent form.

3. Intraocular injection uses local anesthesia (eye drop anesthesia).

4. Normal diet and routine oral medications can be taken on the day of the procedure.

5. The best mode of transportation on the day of the procedure is by public transport; self-cycling or driving is prohibited. It is recommended that a family member accompany the patient.

6. Patients should arrive at the outpatient department, Floor 5, Ophthalmology Surgery Room, at the scheduled time.

## IV. Post-procedure Care

1. After the surgery, the affected eye will be covered with gauze. Please ensure safety and prevent falls.

2. The gauze covering the affected eye should be removed by the patient themselves after four hours. Follow the doctor's prescription to apply medication as scheduled.

3. For two to three days after the injection, avoid getting the eye wet or rubbing the eye to prevent infection.

## V. Post-injection Precautions

1. After injection, patients may experience floating black spots or lines, temporary blurring of vision, or pain at the injection site. These symptoms will gradually improve within a few days after surgery.

2. If severe eye redness, pain, blurred vision, visual field loss, light sensitivity, excessive tearing, or any other discomfort occurs after injection, please visit the hospital immediately for examination.

3. Please return for follow-up examination at the scheduled time after the injection.

For any questions, please contact: Ophthalmology Department, Yida Hospital
