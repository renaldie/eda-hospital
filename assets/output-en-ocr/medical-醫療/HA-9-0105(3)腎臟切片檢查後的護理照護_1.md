### 3. Management of wound pain and hematuria:  

(1) After renal biopsy, if you experience severe pain at the puncture site, severe abdominal pain, pale complexion, night sweats, palpitations, or chest discomfort, please immediately inform the medical staff or return for follow-up.  

(2) Drink adequate fluids to promote urine excretion.  

(3) When you have your first urination, please inform the nursing staff so they can examine it.  

(4) If you experience difficulty in urination, please inform the nursing staff; if necessary, they will assist with catheterization.  

(5) If your urine turns red, please immediately inform the medical staff.  

### 4. Please rest adequately:  

For one to two months after renal biopsy, avoid strenuous activities such as running, bending, lifting heavy objects, riding a bike on bumpy roads, swimming, or soaking in hot springs.

5. Discontinue anticoagulant or antiplatelet medications for 48 hours:  

If you are currently taking antiplatelet or anticoagulant medications, please discontinue them for at least 48 hours after the renal biopsy. Before discharge, discuss with your physician when it is safe to resume medication to ensure your medication safety.  

6. If you have any concerns or questions, please visit the nephrology outpatient clinic or the emergency department of this hospital.
