# Discharge Care Guidance for Victims of Sexual Assault

1. Please pay attention to whether your menstrual cycle is normal, and whether there are any signs of pregnancy, such as nausea, vomiting, or delayed menstruation.

2. Please monitor for any signs of infection, such as fever, lower abdominal pain, itching or burning sensation in the perineal area, or abnormal discharge.

3. Our hospital has conducted testing for sexually transmitted diseases and HIV. Please return for follow-up visits according to your scheduled appointment time to obtain the test results and proceed with further treatment.

4. If you feel depressed, helpless, afraid, anxious, or in pain, you may call the "113 Women and Children Protection Hotline" or directly contact the "Family Violence and Sexual Assault Prevention Center" of the direct-administered municipality or county (city) government. Social workers will provide you with support services.

5. Support channels:

(1) If you have any concerns, please contact the social worker at the Prevention Center for inquiries.
