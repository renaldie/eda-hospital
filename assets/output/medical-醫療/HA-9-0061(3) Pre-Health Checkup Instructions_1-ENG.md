## Computed Tomography (CT) Scans Requiring Contrast Agent Injection

1. If you have diabetes and your examination includes coronary artery angiography (with contrast agent), abdominal or head CT scan (with contrast agent), you must stop taking diabetes medication (metformin), such as Glucophage, Merformin, Glucovance, Actos, etc., 2 days before and 2 days after the examination. Please discuss with your doctor whether you can stop the medication to avoid affecting the examination.

## Friendly Reminders

1. If you have selected anesthesia for a "painless" examination, please do not wear colored nail polish (including gel nails, acrylic nails), do not wear earrings, necklaces, rings, or contact lenses on the day of the exam, and avoid wearing one-piece underwear or pantyhose. Do not bring valuables.

2. For the convenience of female customers, you may wear a wireless sports bra for the examination.

3. Do not perform stool, urine, or Pap smear tests during menstruation. You may submit these tests within 7 days after the examination to the health screening center. If pregnancy is possible, please postpone or avoid X-rays.

4. It is recommended that women aged 30 and above who are sexually active undergo a cervical Pap smear test annually as per the National Health Administration. If the examination includes gynecological ultrasound, you may need to wait until your bladder is full before the test, depending on the situation.
