# Discharge Care Instructions for Sexual Assault Victims

1. Please pay attention to whether your menstrual cycle is normal and whether there are signs of pregnancy, such as nausea, vomiting, or delayed menstruation.

2. Please watch for signs of infection, such as fever, lower abdominal pain, itching in the perineal area, burning sensation in the perineal area, abnormal discharge, or other abnormal conditions.

3. Our hospital has conducted tests for sexually transmitted diseases and HIV for you. Please return for follow-up appointments as scheduled to review the reports and proceed with subsequent treatment.

4. If you feel depressed, helpless, fearful, anxious, or distressed, you may call the "113 Women and Children's Protection Hotline" or personally contact the "Domestic Violence and Sexual Assault Prevention Center" of the municipality or county (city) government, where social workers will provide services for you.

5. Support channels:

(1) If you have any concerns, please contact the prevention center social workers for inquiries.
