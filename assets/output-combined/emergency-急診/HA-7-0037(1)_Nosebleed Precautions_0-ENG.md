## Precautions for Nosebleeds

1. Avoid staying in air-conditioned rooms for long periods or picking your nose to prevent dryness and cracking of the nasal mucosa, which can lead to nosebleeds or infections.

2. When a nosebleed occurs, stopping the bleeding is the priority. Have the patient sit up; if necessary, insert a tissue strip into the nostril. If bleeding does not stop, apply pressure to the bridge of the nose or use ice packs (for trauma patients, do not block the nostrils arbitrarily to prevent blood from entering the airway and causing choking).

3. For patients with hypertension, if blood pressure readings are higher than usual, take emergency antihypertensive medication as directed by a physician, rest, avoid strenuous activities and walking, maintain a semi-recumbent position, and focus on stopping the bleeding.
