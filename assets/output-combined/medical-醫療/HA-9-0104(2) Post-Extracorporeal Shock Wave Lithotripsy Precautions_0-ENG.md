# Post-Extracorporeal Shock Wave Lithotripsy (ESWL) Precautions

1. During the period when small stones are being expelled after the procedure, some people may experience renal colic or mild hematuria for about 2-3 days, which can be controlled with medication.

2. If the patient experiences severe renal colic, high fever, severe hematuria, or other abnormal symptoms, please immediately visit the urology outpatient clinic or emergency department of this hospital for treatment.

3. It is recommended to drink at least 3000cc of water daily, averaging 250cc every two hours.

4. Increase dietary fiber intake: whole wheat bread, oatmeal, fruits.

5. Appropriate beverages: low-salt mineral water, fruit tea, diluted fruit juice, lemon juice, small amounts of milk and yogurt.

6. Less appropriate beverages: black tea, coffee, cola, alcoholic beverages, beer.

7. Avoid obesity.

8. Exercise: climbing stairs, jump rope, jogging.

9. Avoid sweating or other forms of body fluid loss.

10. Avoid stress.

11. Ensure adequate sleep.
