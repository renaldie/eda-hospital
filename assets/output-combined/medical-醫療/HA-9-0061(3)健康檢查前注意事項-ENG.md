## General Precautions

1. Eat a light diet 3 days before the checkup.

2. If the checkup includes functional medicine tests, stop taking vitamins the day before the checkup; women are not advised to check estrogen metabolism during menstruation and can return to the health checkup center within 7 days after the period ends for a supplementary test.

3. The day before the checkup:

(1). Stool sample: Collect the sample according to the stool sample collection instructions. After collection, place it in the green plastic bag and keep it in a cool place.

(2). Starting from midnight, fasting and no water intake (including any fluids) are required.

4. Please arrive at the E-Da Hospital Health Checkup Center on the 6th floor at ___ year ___ month ___ day at ___ o’clock with your ID card, health insurance card, stool sample box, various consent forms, etc., for check-in.

## Medication Precautions

1. For those who take diabetes medication long-term, please do not take the medication on the morning of the checkup day and bring the medication to the health checkup center to take after fasting tests are completed.

2. For those who take chronic disease medications long-term (such as for hypertension, heart disease, and thyroid medications), please follow your doctor’s instructions and bring the medications to the health checkup center.

## Warm Reminders

1. If you have selected anesthesia for a "painless" examination, please do not wear colored nail polish (including gel nails, acrylic nails), do not wear earrings, necklaces, rings, or contact lenses on the day, and do not wear one-piece underwear or tights. Please do not bring valuables.

2. For the convenience of female customers, you may wear a wireless sports bra for the examination.

3. Do not perform stool, urine, or Pap smear tests during menstruation. You can submit these within 7 days after the examination to the health checkup center. If pregnancy is possible, please postpone or avoid X-rays.

4. It is recommended that women over 30 years old who are sexually active undergo a cervical Pap smear test annually as per the National Health Administration. If the checkup includes gynecological ultrasound, you may need to wait until your bladder is full before the examination.
