## Children's acute gastroenteritis precautions

1. During the acute phase, reduce or temporarily stop eating; during fasting, you can moisten your lips with water.

2. Discontinue anti-diarrheal medication if diarrhea is not persistent.

3. Prevent dehydration and pay attention to water and electrolyte replenishment; you may drink sports drinks diluted with equal parts water.

4. Avoid milk and dairy products, oily foods, and spicy foods.

5. After symptoms improve, try giving plain tap water; if no discomfort occurs, gradually introduce small amounts of light foods (such as porridge, rice soup, white toast, fruit juice).

6. If vomiting occurs again, rest for several hours before eating.

7. Prevention methods: wash hands frequently, pay attention to cooking environment, and emphasize food hygiene.

8. Mild diarrhea can be managed at home. However, if vomiting persists, diarrhea continues, dehydration or shock occurs, abdominal pain persists, infants remain irritable and crying, pain shifts to the lower right abdomen, appetite is lost, or if there is confusion, drowsiness, reduced activity, or almost no food intake, seek medical attention promptly.
