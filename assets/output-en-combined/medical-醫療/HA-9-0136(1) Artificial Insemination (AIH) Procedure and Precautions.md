## Notes for Intrauterine Insemination (IUI)

1. Scheduled to inject the HCG ovulation-inducing injection at ___ year ___ month ___ evening at ___ o'clock.

(Do not alter the injection time without authorization. The medication must be stored refrigerated at home.)

2. Scheduled to arrive at the hospital with your ID card and health insurance card by ___ year ___ month ___ morning at ___ o'clock or before ___ o'clock. After semen collection, the sterile container holding the semen must be transported to the Reproductive Medicine Center, Building A, 5th floor, within 2 hours at room temperature. Wait for 1–2 hours. The sperm will be processed and washed, then injected into the uterine cavity. No anesthesia is required, and there is no pain. Simply lie still for 1 hour before getting up and returning home.

3. After IUI, take progesterone supplements after meals starting from ___ year ___ month ___ and continue for 14 consecutive days.

4. On ___ year ___ month ___ (approximately 14 days after IUI), please return to the outpatient clinic for pregnancy testing.

5. After IUI, apart from avoiding intense physical activity, no changes to your usual lifestyle are required.

6. A small number of patients may experience ovarian hyperstimulation syndrome (OHSS) due to stimulation by ovulation-inducing drugs, which often leads to fluid accumulation and weight gain. If you experience severe abdominal distension, difficulty breathing, reduced urination, or loss of appetite, please go to the hospital outpatient clinic immediately for examination.

7. For any questions, please contact the Reproductive Medicine Center at Yida Hospital.
