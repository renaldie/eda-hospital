# Percutaneous Endoscopic Gastrostomy (PEG) Post-Procedure Care Instructions

## 1. Definitions

Patients may require tube feeding due to swallowing difficulties caused by certain diseases. A gastrostomy is an alternative to nasogastric tubes, involving the creation of a stoma on the stomach and abdominal wall, followed by the insertion of a feeding tube.

## 2. General Applicable Cases

1. Patients with stroke.

2. Patients with swallowing difficulties after treatment for head and neck cancer or esophageal cancer.

3. Patients with brain lesions or neuromuscular disorders requiring long-term nasogastric tube placement.

4. Patients who cannot have a nasogastric tube placed.

## Postoperative Care Instructions

## Three. Care and Precautions

1. Start enteral feeding after 24 hours post-surgery, following evaluation by the physician regarding abdominal condition.

2. Pain at the stoma site will persist for several days; appropriate analgesics can be administered.

3. Clean the surrounding area of the stoma daily with normal saline solution 1–2 times, then disinfect with povidone-iodine, and secure with sterile gauze at the stoma site.

4. Daily check for signs of redness, swelling, pain, or swelling at the wound site. If the gauze becomes wet, replace it immediately and keep the wound dry.

5. Flush the tube with 30–50 cc of boiled water before and after feeding.

6. Elevate the head of the bed during and one hour after feeding.
