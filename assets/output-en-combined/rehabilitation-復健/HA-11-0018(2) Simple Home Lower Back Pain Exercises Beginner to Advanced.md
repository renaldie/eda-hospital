# Simple At-Home Lower Back Pain Exercises - Beginner/Advanced

Exercise purpose: To restore daily activities, strengthen core and lower limb muscles, maintain health, and prevent re-injury.

Exercise prescription: Hold for 5–10 seconds, perform 10 repetitions, 4 times daily

## Exercise 1

Wall Slide Exercise: Strengthens back, hip, and thigh muscles

<div style="text-align: center;"><img src="imgs/img_in_image_box_2_389_250_745.jpg" alt="Image" width="20%" /></div>

Stand with a straight back, lean against the wall, feet shoulder-width apart

<div style="text-align: center;"><img src="imgs/img_in_image_box_282_387_505_746.jpg" alt="Image" width="18%" /></div>

Slide your body down to a half-squat position, knees bent slightly more than 90 degrees

## Exercise 2

Heel Raise Exercise: Strengthens hip and thigh muscles

<div style="text-align: center;"><img src="imgs/img_in_image_box_534_410_757_747.jpg" alt="Image" width="18%" /></div>

Sit on a chair, feet on the floor, back flat against the chair back

<div style="text-align: center;"><img src="imgs/img_in_image_box_785_396_980_747.jpg" alt="Image" width="16%" /></div>

<div style="text-align: center;"><img src="imgs/img_in_image_box_990_423_1191_744.jpg" alt="Image" width="16%" /></div>

Extend the calf, raise each foot one at a time to near waist height

## Exercise 3

Glute Bridge Exercise: Strengthens back and glute muscles

<div style="text-align: center;"><img src="imgs/img_in_image_box_71_955_534_1210.jpg" alt="Image" width="38%" /></div>

Lie flat, knees bent, slowly lift the hips, keep knees, hips, and waist in a straight line

## Exercise 4

Trunk Rotation Exercise: Stretches back and glute muscles

<div style="text-align: center;"><img src="imgs/img_in_image_box_666_960_1128_1210.jpg" alt="Image" width="38%" /></div>

Lie flat with knees bent, hands placed on sides, breathe smoothly

<div style="text-align: center;"><img src="imgs/img_in_image_box_68_1319_532_1568.jpg" alt="Image" width="38%" /></div>

Breathe smoothly, slowly lower back down, note the sequence of returning to the bed: upper back → lower back → buttocks

<div style="text-align: center;"><img src="imgs/img_in_image_box_666_1310_1126_1436.jpg" alt="Image" width="38%" /></div>

<div style="text-align: center;"><img src="imgs/img_in_image_box_665_1456_1126_1574.jpg" alt="Image" width="38%" /></div>

Upper back flat on the bed, knees facing left or right, rotate slowly and gently return to the bed surface| | | |
