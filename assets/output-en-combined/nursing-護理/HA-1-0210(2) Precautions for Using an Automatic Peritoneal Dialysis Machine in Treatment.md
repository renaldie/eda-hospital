# Precautions for Treatment with an Automated Peritoneal Dialysis Machine

One. Applicable patients: Patients undergoing treatment with an automated peritoneal dialysis machine.

Two. During treatment with the automated peritoneal dialysis machine, it is not recommended to disconnect the machine's tubing mid-process.

Three. When the following situations occur and it is necessary to disconnect the machine's tubing mid-process, the recommended handling procedures are as follows:

| Occurring Situation | Handling Procedure |
|--------------------|--------------------|
| 1. Experience bodily discomfort requiring medical treatment <img src="imgs/img_in_image_box_327_569_500_723.jpg"> | After disconnecting the machine, immediately go to the hospital for treatment. Contact the peritoneal dialysis nurse the day after. |
| 2. Power outage at night, lasting more than 2 hours <img src="imgs/img_in_image_box_212_842_606_1014.jpg"> | After power is restored, disconnect the body dialysis catheter from the machine tubing, and switch to manual fluid exchange treatment. |
| 3. Using the toilet <img src="imgs/img_in_image_box_292_1123_507_1334.jpg"> | 1. It is not recommended to disconnect the tubing. The machine can be moved outside the bathroom or an adjustable commode chair can be used. <br> 2. If the tubing has already been disconnected, the machine's old tubing must not be reconnected to the body dialysis catheter. |
