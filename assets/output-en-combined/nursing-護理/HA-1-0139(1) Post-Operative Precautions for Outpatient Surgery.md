# Postoperative Care Instructions for Surgical Outpatient Procedures

## ___ Mr./Ms.

## I. Medication Use:

☐ 1. Take oral medications according to the dosage, frequency, and timing specified in the medication bag provided by the doctor. Do not exceed the prescribed amount.

☐ 2. Apply topical medications as instructed; do not take them orally.

## II. Wound Care Methods:

☐ 1. For wounds sutured with absorbable sutures (covered with breathable adhesive tape), simply replace the dressing daily. Do not remove or tear the 3M adhesive tape. No additional topical medication is required.

☐ 2. For wounds sutured with non-absorbable sutures (not covered with breathable adhesive tape and requiring suture removal), apply topical medication as instructed.

☐ 3. Keep the wound dry before the follow-up appointment and avoid getting it wet or submerged to prevent infection.

4. If the wound shows signs of redness, swelling, heat, pain lasting more than two days, or abnormal discharge, please visit the outpatient clinic or emergency department immediately for follow-up and wound evaluation.

□ 5. For wounds with drainage tubes, clean the wound daily with povidone-iodine. The dry gauze covering the drainage tube should be changed when blood or discharge is visible on the outermost layer; otherwise, change it once daily.

☐ 6. If bleeding from the wound persists and does not stop after direct pressure for 15 minutes:

- If still in the hospital, return to the operating room for examination;  
- If already discharged, go to the emergency department for examination.

☐ 7. If elastic gauze or bandages used to secure and compress the wound cause distal limb symptoms such as pallor, coldness, numbness, or significant swelling, loosen the elastic gauze or bandage and gently reapply it. If symptoms do not improve within 10 minutes, please go to the emergency department for evaluation.
