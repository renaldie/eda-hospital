## Dementia - Care Considerations

Dementia is not a normal part of aging. It results from degeneration of the central nervous system, brain injury, cerebrovascular disease, or illness. Symptoms primarily include cognitive impairment, short-term memory loss, and loss of daily living abilities. It may also present with disruptive behaviors, personality changes, delusions, or hallucinations.

Arrange interactive activities and increase physical exercise

During the day, organize activities such as viewing old photos, playing chess, going for walks, sunbathing, and strength training. These activities help improve mood and sleep.

## Strengthen orientation awareness

Keep curtains open during the day to maintain bright lighting. Hang large calendars and clocks, and place familiar items around the room to enhance orientation.

## Care resources

Patients must be medically evaluated and approved by a specialist to apply for benefits such as the Major Illness Card, Mental and Physical Disability Handbook, or foreign caregivers.

They can visit the Dementia Shared Care Center or community service points for dementia care courses, as well as consultations and referrals for daily living care and medical care.

Apply for long-term care resources: Through local health centers or the long-term care hotline 1966. Services include home care, home rehabilitation, assistive device rental or purchase subsidies, day care, transportation services, and respite care.

Prevention of wandering: Facial recognition, personal satellite trackers, love bracelets, fingerprint imprinting, and missing elderly search centers.
