## Food Selection Chart


| Food Category       | Permissible Foods                          | Prohibited Foods                                 |
|---------------------|--------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------|
| Dairy and dairy products | Unsweetened milk and dairy products       | Sweetened dairy products                        |
| Beans, fish, eggs, meat | Tender, lean meats, organs, seafood, eggs. Processed bean products (e.g., soy milk, tofu). | Overcooked or fibrous meats, fried eggs. Unprocessed beans (e.g., red beans, green beans). |
| Vegetables           | Tender, low-fiber vegetables and fruits.  | High-fiber vegetables (e.g., bamboo shoots, celery). Stems, stalks, and old leaves of vegetables. |
| Fruits               | Pitted and seeded fruits. Fresh fruit juice. | Fruits with skin, seeds, or high fiber (e.g., papaya, pineapple). |
| Whole grains and coarse cereals | Whole grains and their products (e.g., rice, noodles). | Glutinous rice. |
| Oils                 | All are permissible.                      | None.                                           |
| Seasonings           | Salt, soy sauce, monosodium glutamate (MSG), vinegar, etc. | Chili, black pepper, mustard, curry, sambal, garlic, etc. |
| Others               | Soda crackers, sugar-free gelatin.        | Desserts, meat broth, fried foods, nuts, strong tea, coffee, alcohol. |

## Gentle Diet

<div style="text-align: center;"><img src="imgs/img_in_image_box_1198_242_1608_850.jpg" alt="Image" width="24%" /></div>## I. Introduction

A gentle diet is a non-irritating, low-fiber, easily digestible diet that provides sufficient nutrition. It reduces the burden on the digestive system of patients with peptic ulcers or gastritis, while ensuring adequate nutritional intake.

<div style="text-align: center;"><img src="imgs/img_in_image_box_128_289_340_498.jpg" alt="Image" width="12%" /></div>


## II. Applicable Populations

(1) Patients with peptic ulcers (gastric ulcers, duodenal ulcers).

(2) Patients with gastritis.

<div style="text-align: center;"><img src="imgs/img_in_image_box_204_736_449_1061.jpg" alt="Image" width="14%" /></div>


## III. General Dietary Principles

(1) Chew food thoroughly, eat at regular times and in fixed portions, with small, frequent meals.

(2) Avoid eating only starch; it is better to combine starch with protein and fat in each meal.

<div style="text-align: center;"><img src="imgs/img_in_image_box_615_256_1044_378.jpg" alt="Image" width="25%" /></div>


(3) The diet should be nutritionally sufficient and non-irritating.

## (4) During acute gastritis:

First, fast for 1-2 days to allow the stomach to rest,  
and drink small amounts of water to prevent thirst.

<div style="text-align: center;"><img src="imgs/img_in_image_box_937_590_1041_706.jpg" alt="Image" width="6%" /></div>


<div style="text-align: center;"><img src="imgs/img_in_image_box_675_714_720_777.jpg" alt="Image" width="2%" /></div>


Adopt a small, frequent meal pattern,  
and gradually introduce milk and liquids.

<div style="text-align: center;"><img src="imgs/img_in_image_box_680_909_723_970.jpg" alt="Image" width="2%" /></div>


<div style="text-align: center;"><img src="imgs/img_in_image_box_889_783_980_901.jpg" alt="Image" width="5%" /></div>


Gradually increase the amount and variety of food, preferring low-fat foods.

<div style="text-align: center;"><img src="imgs/img_in_image_box_939_977_1040_1087.jpg" alt="Image" width="6%" /></div>


(5) Peptic ulcers are divided into three stages according to the treatment protocol:

## Stage One (from bleeding to hemostasis, lasting 2-3 days):

1. Clear liquid diet.  
2. Prepare low-sugar, oil-free foods into liquid form.

<div style="text-align: center;"><img src="imgs/img_in_image_box_1327_234_1482_444.jpg" alt="Image" width="9%" /></div>


âœ— Stage Two (2-3 days after hemostasis to recovery period): liquid, soft foods, or gentle diet.

## Stage Three (recovery period):

At this stage, the diet is similar to a regular diet. Unless the patient feels discomfort, they should try to consume a variety of foods.
